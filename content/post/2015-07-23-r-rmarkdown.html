Abstract

This research aims at discovering the change of the situation of daily shelters in ON due to the strike of COVID-19. The analysis is based on the comparison between the data from year 2020 and year 2019, and will especially focus on the changes in occupancy rate. The most important finding is that the average occupancy rate has decreased, while the situation of overwhelmed shelter is even more severe.

Introduction

In year 2020, due to the strike of COVID-19, the Ontario government has required everyone to keep the social distance and reduce the frequency of going out to stop the spread of COVID-19. That means, all the homeless people on the street has to go to a shelter. That makes me get curious: will there be more shelters in Ontario to host all the homeless people compared to last year? Are there enough spots in those shelters? To answer the questions, I did a statistical research and find out that there might not be enough shelters in Ontario.

From the analysis, I find out that the number of shelters that are under performance this year is even less than the number of last year(2019), along with a decrease in the occupancy rate. The average occupancy rate in 2019 is 0.9559, while that in 2020 is 0.7981. ALthough the situation of a overwhelmed shelter has existed since last year, the highest occupancy rate in ON this year for a single shelter has arrived 4.05, which is almost 3 times of that in last year. This means, the use ratio of the shelters is below the average during the period of COVID-19, and there are not enough available spots provided in the shelter.

During the process of the analyzing the occupancy rate, we are using the data from OPENDATA Toronto. And they collect the information of all the shelters in Ontario everyday at 4 a.m. However, since I am doing the research in the middle of 2020, the data I am using only covered that past 9 months, that may cause some mistake when I use the data to do the comparison and analysis. For example, there might be more homeless people in the shelter in winter due to the weather, and the average ratio might be underestimated with the use of current data. Another possible error is, when I am doing the research, neither the data from 2020 or the data from 2019 is selected randomly, I literally used all the data points that I am provided with. The conclusion that I can draw from the current data is there is not enough spots in the shelters of ON for the past 9 months, and the government should invest more to make sure that all the homeless can stay safe in a shelter during this special period . I will continue to do the research and decide how many more shelters and spots should be provided in the future.

From the chart and the histograms above, it is not hard to conclude that the average occupancy rate in year 2020 is about 20% lower than that in 2020. In specific, the histogram for year 2020 is hardly right-skewed, while we could say the histogram for year 2019 is slightly right-skewed. Although they both peak at a occupancy ratio=1, there are more extreme data points in year 2020 compared to year 2019. This could lead to a conclusion that although the shelters almost reach their maximum capacity in both year and get overwhelmed to some extent, the situation in 2020 may be more extreme, since there are more outliers.

In general, the situation we are facing right now is some shelters are not fully used, while some others are extremely overloaded. The possible reason for this might be that people can not commute conveniently due to the COVID-19, so they are forced to stay in the certain area. However, even if we are only looking at the data in year 2019, we cans say that there is no enough shelter spots in ON, to fulfill the society’s needs for shelter spots, the government needs to make more investment.
